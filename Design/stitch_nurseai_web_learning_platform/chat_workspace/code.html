Can you explain the mechanism of action for ACE inhibitors in a clinical context?

NurseAI Tutor

Certainly! ACE Inhibitors (Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme inhibitors) work by blocking the conversion of Angiotensin I to the potent vasoconstrictor Angiotensin II.

Clinical Impact:

  • Vasodilation: Decreases peripheral vascular resistance (afterload).
  • Sodium/Water Excretion: Reduces aldosterone secretion, aiding in fluid volume reduction.
  • Cardiac Remodeling: Prevents adverse structural changes post-MI.
